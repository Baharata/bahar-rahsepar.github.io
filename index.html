Research Projects

I beleive in collaborative open science and enjoy wokring with other people. Here are some of the projects I had the privellege of working with passionate group of people.

Modulation of Memory Neurons

Memory processing is the underlying cause for various psychiatric disorders. My dissertation project aims to identify effective strategies to modulate memories. I lead a collaboration effort between our lab and Steve Ramirez group and work with a group of graduate and undergraduate students on this project. I use two-photon calcium imaging, real-time optogenetics and molecular engineering tools for this project.

Electrophysiology of Neuronal Sub-types

Biophysics of neurons determines their dynamics and contribution to brain computation. Using in-vitro whole cell patch-calmp recording, it's possible to characterize electrical properties of neurons. I have applied this technique differentiate between excitatory and inhibitory neurons. Also, in collaboration with Professor Xue Han, we have identified a novel sub-type of motor cortex, expressing excitatory markers but exhibiting inhibitory neuron physiology.

Real-time Seizure Detection Algorithm

Deep Brain Stimulation (DBS) has proven successful in treatment of different conditions, including epilepsy. In order to be able to intervene more effectively, it is required to tie the stimulation with onset of seizures. For my undergraduate thesis in Professor Robert Gross lab, I developed a real-time seizure detection algorithm, for optogenetic suppression of seizures in rat model of epilepsy.
