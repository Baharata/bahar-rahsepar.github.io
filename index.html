I hold a Bachelor of Science in Biomedical Engineering from Georgia Insitute of Technology and currently working on my PhD in BME at Boston University. I am always working to sharpen my advanced data analytics skills and have participated in multiple short courses and summer schools.

About me

I am a neuroengineer by training and passionate about biotechnology innovation to improve quality of life for patients with various neurological disorders. Inside the lab I combine experimental and computational methods to study memory processing in the mammalian brain. Outside of the lab, I am involved in the biotechnology community in greater Boston area to identify avenues to fulfill promises of basic science research. Always exploring and open to new oppurtunities for biotechnology innovation.

Learn More

Research

Memory processing is the underlying cause for various psychiatric disorders. My current research is focused on identifying effective strategies to modulate memories with perspective of novel intervention strategies in treatment of these diseases. Outside of my main research, I have a track record of various collaborations to better understand neuroscientific principles underlying activity of mamalian brain. I beleive in open and reproducable science, and love working with other people.
